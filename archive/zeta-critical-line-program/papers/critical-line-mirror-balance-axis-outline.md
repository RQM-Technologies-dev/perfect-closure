# Outline: The Critical Line as the Mirror-Balance Axis

## One-Paragraph Summary

This draft frames the completed Riemann zeta function $\xi(s)$ as a mirror-symmetric system under $s\mapsto 1-s$, with the critical line $\Re(s)=\tfrac12$ as the fixed axis of reflection. After recentering by $s=\tfrac12+z$, the identity $\Xi(z)=\Xi(-z)$ makes the symmetry explicit as evenness. The main interpretive proposal is that nontrivial zeros should be analyzed as candidate **Perfect Closure** points, where mirror-paired contributions satisfy exact phase cancellation, equal magnitude weighting, and zero residual drift. The draft does not claim to prove RH; instead it proposes a constraint-based strategy: define analytic closure functionals and show that off-axis imbalance $\delta=\Re(s)-\tfrac12\neq0$ prevents simultaneous satisfaction of all closure conditions.

## Open Proof Obligations

- Provide a canonical decomposition for mirrored contributions $A(s),A^*(s)$ that is not ad hoc.
- Formalize “equal-magnitude mirror weighting” in a decomposition-independent way.
- Define drift at/near zeros without branch ambiguity for logarithms.
- Prove that zero events of $\xi$ imply a strict closure condition, not merely vanishing value.
- Prove an off-axis obstruction theorem: if $\delta\neq0$, at least one closure condition fails.
- Establish compatibility with known symmetry classes of zeros (conjugate/reflected quartets) without circularity.
- Connect local closure constraints to global zero-distribution tools (argument principle, explicit formula, or entire-function products).
