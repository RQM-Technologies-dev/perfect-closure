# Two-Route Closure Model

## Purpose

This note isolates the simplest possible closure mechanism:
two conjugate lightlike phase routes with opposite spatial momentum.

It is a toy kinematic bridge, not a full particle microdynamics model.

## Two equal lightlike routes

Define

\[
p_+^\mu=\left(\frac{E}{c},+\mathbf p\right),\qquad
p_-^\mu=\left(\frac{E}{c},-\mathbf p\right).
\]

Each route is individually null:

\[
E^2-p^2c^2=0.
\]

So each component remains a free lightlike route at local level.

## Closed total four-momentum

Summing the two routes gives

\[
P^\mu=p_+^\mu+p_-^\mu=\left(\frac{2E}{c},0\right).
\]

Hence the invariant mass of the closed pair is

\[
M=\frac{2E}{c^2}.
\]

Interpretation:

> Closed light is not free light "sitting still."  
> It is oppositely routed lightlike phase whose directional motion closes, leaving rest energy as invariant mass.

## Quaternionic conjugate-phase form

Use a unit pure-imaginary quaternion \(\mathbf u\) with \(\mathbf u^2=-1\), and define conjugate routes

\[
q_+(\phi)=e^{\mathbf u\phi},\qquad
q_-(\phi)=e^{-\mathbf u\phi}.
\]

Then

\[
q_+q_-=1.
\]

Interpretation:

The conjugate phase routes close to identity. Directional displacement cancels, but the enclosed phase energy remains as invariant rest mass.

## Role in the repository

This two-route closure model sits between:

1. free-vs-closed light ontology, and
2. shell-based models such as hydrogen locking on \(S^3\times\mathbb R_s\).

It is the minimal mechanistic bridge that makes the thesis physically legible.
