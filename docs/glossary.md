# Perfect Closure Series Glossary

## Perfect Closure
A vanishing-residual or invariant-return condition within a chosen formal system. In this series, it appears as either a zeta residual zero, an operator-kernel condition, or a mass-shell norm identity.

## square-root mirror
The critical-line pairing in which prime routes have equal amplitude \(p^{-1/2}\) and opposite phase direction, e.g. \(p^{-(1/2\pm it)}\).

## complex spectral slice
The complex-line view of a broader spectral structure, represented here by \(\Xi(t)=\xi(\tfrac12+it)\) and conditions such as \(\Xi(t_n)=0\).

## quaternionic closure slice
A slice \(\mathbb C_{\mathbf u}\subset\mathbb H\) with \(\mathbf u^2=-1\), used to represent axis-free closure geometry; the complex critical line is treated as one slice of this family.

## closure eigenvalue
A value \(t\) satisfying an eigen-equation such as \(\mathcal A_{\mathbf u}\chi_t=t\chi_t\), which may then be tested against selector and lift conditions.

## zeta closure selector
The operator \(\mathcal Z_{\mathbf u}=\Xi(\mathcal A_{\mathbf u})\), used to select kernel modes associated with zeta closure ordinates.

## mass-shell norm
The invariant biquaternionic relation \(P\bar P=m^2c^2\), where positive norm corresponds to massive states and zero norm to null propagation.

## closure-conversion length \(L_\ast\)
A scale converting internal closure eigenvalues into physical curvature or mass scale, e.g. \(m_n=\hbar|t_n|/(cL_\ast)\).

## resolved Perfect Closure
A consistency triple \((t_n,L_\ast,m_n)\) in which zeta slice, eigenmode condition, and mass-shell norm relation all hold together within the framework.

## non-claim boundary
A stated limit on interpretation, including: no proof of RH, no claim that zeta zeros are particles, and no claim that known particle masses have already been derived.
