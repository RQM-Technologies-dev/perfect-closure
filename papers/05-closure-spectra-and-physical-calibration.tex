\begin{abstract}
This paper states the calibration problem for closure-spectrum models and defines evidence and falsification criteria.
\end{abstract}
\section{Calibration equation}
\[
m_n=\frac{\hbar}{cL_*}|t_n|.
\]
Without a derived $L_*$, this remains a formal bridge.
\section{Evidence criteria}
\[
\frac{m_a}{m_b}\approx\frac{t_m}{t_n},
\qquad
L_*=\frac{\hbar t_n}{m_nc}
\]
should hold in physically justified assignments with stability across families.
\section{Falsification criteria}
No stable $L_*$, ad hoc reassignment, or absence of independent prediction counts against the program.
