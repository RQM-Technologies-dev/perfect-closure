# Perfect Closure as the Light-to-Mass Thesis

**John Van Geem / RQM Technologies**  
*Preprint - May 2026*

## Abstract

This paper restates Perfect Closure with a new center: stable mass is closed light-like phase. Free light is modeled as non-closing null phase propagation, while mass is modeled as a self-closed quaternionic standing-wave resonance. The argument is structural, not reductionist: mass is interpreted as a closure state of phase rather than as an independently fundamental substance. The paper defines the ontological layer, introduces the free-light/closed-light distinction, and sets strict claim boundaries: no RH proof claim, no replacement of standard physics, and no first-principles derivation of all constants.

## 1. Introduction

The central proposition of this series is:

> **It is all light; light that has closure becomes mass.**

This statement is not a slogan replacing physics. It is an organizing thesis that reframes the difference between radiation and matter in closure terms.

- Free radiation corresponds to non-closing phase propagation.
- Massive structure corresponds to phase that has self-closed into a persistent standing pattern.

The motivation is to make the closure program physically legible without requiring Riemann-zeta-centered framing. Earlier zeta-critical-line work is retained in archive as historical analogy, but this series no longer treats RH-adjacent structure as the main doorway.

## 2. Ontological Core

Define **Perfect Closure** as completed phase return under a given conjugacy law. In physical language:

\[
\text{free light} \rightarrow \text{non-closing null phase},
\]
\[
\text{mass} \rightarrow \text{closed light-like phase}.
\]

This distinction can be read through invariant behavior:

- Null propagation: no rest-frame closure state.
- Massive mode: stable closure supports an invariant rest-frame norm.

The key claim is qualitative and structural:

> Mass is phase closure, not material substance.

This claim is compatible with standard relativistic and quantum descriptions when treated as interpretive geometry layered on top of established equations.

## 3. Closure States and Stability

Closure language separates three regimes:

1. **Open propagation:** phase does not return; transport dominates.
2. **Transient closure:** partial return occurs but is not dynamically stable.
3. **Stable closure shell:** standing-wave return persists and supports quantized spectra.

The research target is regime (3): stable shell closure with spectral consequences.

## 4. Mathematical Posture

This paper does not attempt a full dynamical derivation from fundamental field equations. Instead it sets a constrained scaffold:

- geometric closure language;
- operator/spectrum bridge for quantized shells;
- calibration discipline for empirical contact.

In this scaffold, hydrogen is introduced in later papers as the first calibrated closure-shell example.

## 5. Claim Boundaries

The current thesis explicitly does **not** claim:

1. a proof of the Riemann Hypothesis;
2. a derivation of all physical constants from first principles;
3. replacement of QED/QM/relativistic formalism;
4. that zeta-zero matching experiments establish physical mass law.

It does claim that closure-shell language can be formulated rigorously enough to build falsifiable bridge models.

## 6. Relation to the Series

Paper sequence:

- **Paper 1 (this paper):** Ontological and structural thesis.
- **Paper 2:** Quaternionic standing-wave geometry and mass-shell form.
- **Paper 3:** Hydrogen shell-locking bridge on \(S^3\times\mathbb R_s\).

## 7. Conclusion

Perfect Closure is recentered as a light-to-mass thesis: free light is non-closing phase propagation; mass is stable closed phase. The contribution of Paper 1 is conceptual precision plus claim discipline, preparing a geometric and spectral bridge rather than a proof-theoretic claim.
