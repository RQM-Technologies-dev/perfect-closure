\begin{abstract}
This paper gives the main synthesis of the series. The same value $t_n$ appears in three aligned forms:
\[
\Xi(t_n)=0,\qquad
\mathcal A_{\mathbf u}\chi_t=t\chi_t,\qquad
P\bar P=\left(\frac{\hbar t_n}{L_*}\right)^2.
\]
So a zeta zero is read as a complex spectral trace of a quaternionic closure eigenvalue, while the mass shell is the physical norm realization after scale conversion by $L_*$. This is a formal bridge, not a proof of RH and not a particle-mass derivation.
\end{abstract}

\section{Internal scale operator and eigenmodes}
Let $\rho>0$ and define
\[
\mathcal A_{\mathbf u}=\mathbf u\left(\rho\frac{d}{d\rho}+\frac12\right),\qquad \mathbf u^2=-1.
\]
Take
\[
\chi_t(\rho)=\rho^{-1/2-\mathbf u t}.
\]
Then
\[
\mathcal A_{\mathbf u}\chi_t=t\chi_t.
\]

\section{Complex zeta-slice condition and selector}
Define
\[
\Xi(t)=\xi\!\left(\frac12+it\right).
\]
The complex spectral-trace condition is
\[
\Xi(t_n)=0.
\]
Define
\[
\mathcal Z_{\mathbf u}=\Xi(\mathcal A_{\mathbf u}).
\]
Then
\[
\Xi(\mathcal A_{\mathbf u})\chi_{t_n}=0
\]
whenever $\Xi(t_n)=0$.

\section{Complex Spectral Slices of Quaternionic Mass Shells}
The central synthesis is that $t_n$ has two faces. In the zeta sector, $t_n$ appears through the complex residual $\Xi(t_n)=0$. In the quaternionic sector, it appears as the eigenvalue of $\mathcal A_{\mathbf u}$. In the physical sector, after the scale map $L_*$, it appears as a mass-shell norm $P\bar P=(\hbar t_n/L_*)^2$. Thus, zeta zeros may be interpreted as complex spectral traces of quaternionic closure eigenvalues, not as particles themselves.

\begin{remark}
The zeta zero is not the mass shell itself. It is the complex spectral trace of a closure eigenvalue. The mass shell is the physical norm realization after scale conversion.
\end{remark}
