## Abstract

This paper advances the thesis that Perfect Closure is native to quaternionic geometry and that the classical critical line $\operatorname{Re}(s)=\tfrac12$ is its complex trace. We define quaternionic critical slices $q_u(t)=\tfrac12+ut$ across unit imaginary directions $u^2=-1$, distinguish affine slices from unit phase elements, and derive a six-step closure gate at real part $\tfrac12$ in phase form. We then formalize a slice-lifted completed zeta representation and a quaternionic closure residual. The proposal is interpretive and programmatic: it does not prove the Riemann Hypothesis and does not replace analytic number theory.

## 2. Quaternionic and Complex Slices

Let $u$ be a unit imaginary quaternion with $u^2=-1$. Define the quaternionic critical slice
$$
q_u(t)=\frac12+ut, \qquad t\in\mathbb R.
$$
Define the corresponding complex slice
$$
\mathbb C_u=\{a+ub:\;a,b\in\mathbb R,\;u^2=-1\}.
$$
The usual complex critical line
$$
s=\frac12+it
$$
is the special case $u=i$.

Hence $\operatorname{Re}(s)=\tfrac12$ is not the whole closure geometry; it is the complex trace of the quaternionic hyperplane $\operatorname{Re}(q)=\tfrac12$.

## 3. Affine Slice vs. Unit Phase Form

Two different objects must be separated:

- **Affine critical slice:** $q=\tfrac12+ut$.
- **Unit phase form:** $Q=e^{u\phi}=\cos\phi+u\sin\phi$.

The affine form organizes spectral location. The phase form organizes multiplicative closure behavior.

## 4. Six-Step Closure Gate at Real Part $1/2$

If $\operatorname{Re}(Q)=\tfrac12$, then
$$
\cos\phi=\frac12\quad\Rightarrow\quad\phi=\frac\pi3.
$$
So
$$
Q=e^{u\pi/3},\qquad Q^3=e^{u\pi}=-1,\qquad Q^6=e^{u2\pi}=1.
$$
This yields a six-step multiplicative closure law and identifies $\operatorname{Re}(q)=\tfrac12$ as a closure gate in quaternionic phase geometry.

This six-step closure is already visible on the ordinary complex unit circle. Quaternionic space does not invent it; it makes the closure law axis-free across all imaginary directions.

## 5. Slice-Lifted Completed Zeta and Residual

Define a slice-lifted completed zeta quantity by
$$
\xi_H\!\left(\frac12+ut\right)
=
\operatorname{Re}\,\xi\!\left(\frac12+it\right)
+u\,\operatorname{Im}\,\xi\!\left(\frac12+it\right).
$$
Define the quaternionic closure residual
$$
C_{H,u}(t)=\left\|\xi_H\!\left(\frac12+ut\right)\right\|^2.
$$
A Perfect Closure event is
$$
C_{H,u}(t)=0.
$$
The classical residual $|\xi(\tfrac12+it)|^2$ is then one slice representation of this quaternionic residual.
